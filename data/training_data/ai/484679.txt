 Dear State Senator,

Ks you consider the system for electing the President of the United States, I urge you to keep or modify the current Electoral College system to ensure a fair and democratic process. While some argue that the Electoral College is outdated and unfair, I believe there are compelling reasons to maintain or modify it.

Firstly, the Electoral College ensures a winnertakesall outcome, preventing disputes over the popular vote. In 2000, the presidential election was decided by a mere 537 votes in Florida, highlighting the potential for a tie in the national popular vote. The Electoral College provides a clear outcome, reducing the likelihood of a disputed election. (Plume, 2004)

Secondly, the Electoral College encourages candidates to campaign in all states, rather than focusing solely on swing states. This leads to a more inclusive and representative campaign, as candidates must appeal to a broader range of voters. Additionally, the candidate who receives the most popular votes has a higher likelihood of winning the Electoral College, as the winnertakeall system in each state ensures a significant plurality of electoral votes. (Poster, 2012)

Thirdly, the Electoral College gives the residents of smaller states a voice in the election process. Without the Electoral College, larger states would have a disproportionate influence on the outcome, as their voters would have more weight in the electoral college than those from smaller states. The Electoral College helps to balance the political power of different states, ensuring that all voters have an equal say in the election. (Poster, 2012)
